# Find a stretch assignment with the Skills agent

> Stop guessing what to work on next. Hand the Skills agent your current strengths and where you want
> to grow, and get back concrete stretch assignments, internal openings, and the one skill that would
> open the most doors.

**Stage:** First-Party Agents · **For:** End user, Manager · **Level:** Starter · **Time:** 10 min

## When to use this
You're ready for more — a bigger project, a different team, a step toward a role you've been eyeing — but
"look at the internal jobs board" is a weak plan. The **Skills agent** is a newer first-party capability
that reasons over your skills profile and your org's opportunities to suggest *specific* next moves:
stretch assignments, projects, mentors, and internal roles that build toward where you want to go.

Use it when you want a deliberate next step instead of waiting for one to appear.

## What you'll need
- **M365 Copilot license** with the **Skills** capability enabled in your org (it draws on your Viva /
  Microsoft 365 profile and the opportunities your org has published)
- A rough sense of **where you want to grow** — a role, a domain, or a skill
- Five honest minutes to describe what you're good at and what you actually enjoy

## Try it now — the prompt
Open the Skills agent and ask it to match growth areas to real opportunities:

```
I'm a [current role] strong in [2–3 skills] and I want to grow toward [target role
or skill]. Based on my profile and what's available in my org, suggest: three
stretch assignments or projects I could take on now, any internal roles worth
exploring, and the single skill that would open the most doors — plus how I'd build it.
```

**Why this works:** anchoring on **where you are**, **where you want to go**, and asking for *specific,
available* next steps (not generic advice) turns a fuzzy "help me grow" into a shortlist you can act on
this week — and naming the highest-leverage skill keeps you focused.

## Step by step
1. **Open the Skills agent.** Find it in the Microsoft 365 Copilot app or your Viva experience, wherever
   your org surfaced skills and growth tools.
2. **Describe your strengths and your target.** The more honest the input — including what you *enjoy*, not
   just what you're good at — the better the matches.
3. **Review the suggestions and pressure-test them:**
   ```
   For the stretch assignment you'd recommend first, what skills would it build,
   who should I talk to, and how do I pitch taking it on to my manager?
   ```
4. **Turn it into a conversation.** Ask the agent to draft talking points for your next 1:1 so the growth
   move becomes a real plan, not a private wish.

## Make it better
Go from a list to a plan:
- **Sequence it.** "Order these by what builds the target skill fastest, and give me a 90-day plan."
- **Find the people.** "Who in my org has the role I'm targeting, and what should I ask them in a coffee chat?"
- **Close a specific gap.** "I'm missing [skill] — find learning and a project that would build it."

> **📚 Learn more.** The [Microsoft Viva Learning](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/viva/learning/)
> documentation and the [Agents in Microsoft 365 hub](https://adoption.microsoft.com/en-us/ai-agents/agents-in-microsoft-365/)
> describe how skills and growth capabilities work across Microsoft 365.

## Watch out for
- **It sees what your org has published.** Suggestions depend on the opportunities and skills data your org
  maintains. Thin data, thinner matches — treat gaps as a nudge to update your own profile.
- **Availability varies.** The Skills capability depends on your org's Viva/Copilot configuration. If you
  don't see it, the same framing works as a thinking exercise in Copilot Chat.
- **It suggests; you decide.** The agent points at options. Whether a stretch is right for you — and your
  life — is your call.

## Where this leads (the ramp)
Once an agent is helping you plan *forward*, the natural companion is one that helps you account for the
work you've *already done* — the raw material for reviews and self-assessments.
