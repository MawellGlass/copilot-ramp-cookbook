From your first chat to production agents

Get started with Microsoft Copilot — then grow all the way to building agents.

Most people stall at “I have Copilot, now what?” This cookbook lays out a natural ramp: start by using Copilot, get comfortable letting agents do work for you, then take the leap to building your own — first in Agent Builder, then in Copilot Studio.

Who it’s for: end users who want a first win · champions rolling Copilot out to a team · managers building the case · makers ready to build their first agent · IT/admins enabling each stage safely.

›Turn a meeting into tracked follow-ups

Turn a meeting into tracked follow-ups

One-line value. Walk out of any meeting and have decisions, owners, and due dates captured in under five minutes — without re-watching the recording.
Stage · Copilot ChatFor · End user, ChampionLevel · StarterTime · 5 min

When to use this

You just finished a 45-minute project sync. Three decisions were made, half a dozen tasks got handed out, and two things were left hanging — all of it living in your memory and a wall of chat. Instead of scrubbing the recording tonight, you let Copilot read the meeting and hand you a clean list while it’s fresh.

This is the single best “first win” for a new Copilot user: low-risk, instantly useful, and it shows the magic on day one.

What you’ll need

  • M365 Copilot license (Copilot in Teams / Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat)
  • The meeting had transcription or recording on (Copilot reads the transcript, not the audio)
  • You were an attendee, or have access to the meeting recap

Try it now — the prompt

Summarize the meeting "[meeting name]" from [today/this morning]. Give me:
- Decisions made
- Action items, each with an owner and a due date
- Any open questions that were not resolved
Format the action items as a table I can paste into an email.

Why this prompt works: it names the meeting (so Copilot grounds on the right transcript), asks for specific outputs instead of a vague “summary,” and dictates the output format (“a table I can paste”). Specific asks + named format = dramatically better results.

📚Learn more: want a deeper library of patterns like this? The Copilot Prompt Gallery has paste-and-go prompts for every M365 app, and the prompting principles guide explains why they work.

Step by step

  1. Open Copilot in Teams. From the meeting’s recap page, the Copilot side panel, or Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat at office.com. You’ll see the chat box.
📷 Screenshot 01 — Copilot side panel open next to a Teams meeting recap.
  1. Paste the prompt (swap in your meeting name). Copilot reads the transcript and returns a structured summary — a decisions list, an action-item table with owners and dates, and an open-questions list.
📷 Screenshot 02 — the prompt typed into the Copilot chat box.
📷 Screenshot 03 — Copilot’s structured response: decisions, action-item table, open questions.
  1. Sanity-check owners and dates. Skim the table. A missing due date or wrong owner is expected — you’ll fix it next.
  2. Refine in plain language:
Any action item without a due date — set it to end of this week.
And the second item should be owned by Priya, not me.

Copilot updates the table in place.

📷 Screenshot 04 — the refined table after the follow-up prompt.

Make it better

Once the table is right, chain follow-ups to turn a summary into progress:

  • Draft a short email to each owner with just their action items and due dates.
  • Flag any action item that depends on another one finishing first.
  • Rewrite the open questions as agenda items for next week’s sync.

Watch out for

  • No transcript, no summary. If recording/transcription was off, Copilot has nothing to read. Turn it on at the start of meetings going forward.
  • Owner attribution can slip. Copilot infers who owns what from who spoke — verify before you send. The 30-second check is worth it.
  • Name the meeting precisely. “This morning’s sync” works if you only had one; otherwise use the exact title.

Where this leads (the ramp)

You’re ready for Stage 2.

Doing this after every meeting is the tell. The first-party Facilitator agent generates recaps and follow-ups automatically — no prompt required — and agents in Teams channels can post the action items for the team. You’ve just learned by hand what an agent will soon do on autopilot.
